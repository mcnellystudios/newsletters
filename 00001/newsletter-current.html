Peterborough Real Estate Market Update – March 2026

March was a busy and encouraging month in the Peterborough City market — 72 homes sold across all property types, with detached homes leading the way at 59 sales and an average sale price of $595,701, while the overall average across all home types came in at $570,485 with a median of $544,500, and sellers were receiving a strong 98% of their asking price on average — a sign that well-priced homes are still attracting real buyer interest — with properties spending an average of just 44 days on market, which, when you consider the broader county context where some rural areas like Otonabee-South Monaghan and Trent Lakes are sitting closer to 82–87 days, tells us that the city core continues to be where demand is most concentrated; and while we don't have the prior six months of reports directly in front of us right now, the year-to-date 2026 numbers offer some helpful context — through March, Peterborough City has seen 181 total sales at an average of $553,571, suggesting March's higher average reflects a seasonal pick-up in activity that's typical of spring markets, and this aligns well with the Bank of Canada's recent rate environment, where the policy rate has been on a gradual easing path through late 2025 and into 2026, giving buyers a bit more breathing room on mortgage affordability and nudging some of those fence-sitters back into the market — so whether you're thinking about listing this spring or finally ready to make your move as a buyer, I'd love to chat about what all of this means for your specific situation, so feel free to reach out anytime — no pressure, just a good conversation! ☕
